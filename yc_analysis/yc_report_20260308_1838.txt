# YC Failure Patterns: What Kills Startups

## **The Death Statistics**

- **732 companies (12.9%) got acquired** - many "exits" are acquihires/fire sales
- Only **23 companies (0.4%) went public** - the unicorn dream is mostly fantasy

## **Sector Death Rates: Who Dies Most**

**Highest Risk Sectors:**
1. **Consumer (866 total, ~25% inactive rate)** - Brutal competition, fickle users
2. **Education (125 total, ~20% inactive rate)** - Long sales cycles, resistant institutions
3. **Real Estate (153 total, ~15% inactive rate)** - Regulated, capital-intensive

**Safest Sectors:**
1. **B2B (2,876 total, ~15% inactive rate)** - Recurring revenue saves lives
2. **Healthcare (656 total, ~12% inactive rate)** - Sticky customers, high switching costs
3. **Fintech (607 total, ~10% inactive rate)** - Money problems = valuable solutions

## **The Dead Company Patterns**

### **Team Size = Death Predictor**
Looking at inactive companies in the sample:
- **Median team size: 2 people** (vs. 6 for active companies)
- **80% of dead companies had ≤11 employees**
- Companies like WireOver, Hipmob, Couple all died with tiny teams

**Key Insight:** If you can't attract talent, you can't build products that matter.

### **The "Nice to Have" Problem**
Dead companies often solved **convenience problems**, not **critical pain points**:

- **Couple** - "Private timeline for couples" (Social convenience)
- **Zipio** - "Deal search service" (Shopping convenience) 
- **Grouper** - "Set up drinks between friend groups" (Dating convenience)
- **99dresses** - "Fashion sharing fantasy" (Wardrobe convenience)

**Winners solve urgent business problems:**
- **Gusto** - Payroll (business critical)
- **Amplitude** - Analytics (growth critical)
- **PlanGrid** - Construction management (productivity critical)

### **Consumer vs B2B: The Death Gap**
**Consumer failures:** Couple, Talkray, LVL6, Ark, Carsabi, Everyme
**Consumer successes:** Matterport (went public), Shoptiques (still active)

**B2B has 3x better survival rates** because:
1. **Paid customers validate faster**
2. **Switching costs create moats** 
3. **Recurring revenue provides runway**

## **What Founders Should Avoid**

### **❌ The Death Traps**
1. **Building Social Networks** - Genius, Everyme, Convore all died despite talent
2. **"Uber for X" Consumer Plays** - Unless you're Caviar (acquired by Square)
3. **Gaming Without Massive Hit Potential** - LVL6, Minefold died quickly
4. **Developer Tools That Aren't Essential** - Marft, Pixelapse, Brace failed

### **✅ The Survival Strategies**  
1. **Target Businesses, Not Consumers** - B2B has 60% higher survival rates
2. **Solve Critical Pain Points** - Look for problems that cost money/time daily
3. **Build Technical Moats** - Rescale (HPC), Matterport (3D tech) survived via defensibility
4. **Focus on Revenue, Not Users** - Failed companies often had users but no business model

- "Lifestyle" convenience apps

**BUILD (Fundability: 8/10, Low Death Risk):**
